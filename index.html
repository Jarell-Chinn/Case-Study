Compassionate Service Society Nonprofit Website Redesign

Introduction

The Compassionate Service Society (CSS) is a non-profit organization based in Southern California, USA. Founded on Buddhist principles, CSS offers a variety of programs that seek to unfold the light of wisdom and compassion innate in all of us. Their initiatives include meditation retreats, integral tai chi classes, vegetarian cooking workshops, as well as the annual World Peace Gathering.

User Research

Proto Persona

Ray Hokama

Age: 63

Goals and Needs

  • She wants to find a community with the same interests
  • Wants to find therapeutic sessions that help with her spiritual health
  • Wants to give back to the community

Behavioral Demographic

  • Buddhist
  • Vegetarian
  • Elderly person (55+)
  • Just retired
  • Has lots of free time on her hands
  • Enjoys meditation

Pain Points

  • She feels stiff in her body and wants to feel more relaxed and flexible
  • Not tech-savvy so has a hard time navigating new technology
  • Has a hard time finding a close-knit community with similar interests

User Interview Findings

  • Too many different colors and fonts used
  • No consistent branding
  • No registration section
  • A confusing navigation

User Insight

Users that are looking for a community to join, seek a straightforward and efficient path to discover programs and navigate websites online.

Problem Statement

The Compassionate Service Society website aims to inform visitors about the organization and inspire them to get involved in their initiatives.

We have observed that the current navigation system is confusing and prevents users from easily accessing information about upcoming events and programs. This hinders their ability to successfully engage with the organization.

How might we improve the navigation system to make their website easier to locate critical information about programs and events?
